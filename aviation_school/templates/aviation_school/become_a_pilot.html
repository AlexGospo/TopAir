Private Pilot License

The private license is usually obtained in 3 to 6 months and allows the pilot to bring passengers. Compensation for flying is not permitted. However, you are allowed to share the costs with passengers. You can also fly abroad according to the regulations in force. You can add the qualifications of night flight, over-the-top, multi-engine and instrument flight.

    Prerequisites
    • 17 years old
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1 or 3
    Ground Training
    • Theoretical ground course 40 hrs
    • Preparatory instructions & presentation 15 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Practice flights in dual control flight 36 hours
    • Solo practice flights 13.5 hours

Commercial Pilot License

The process to obtain a commercial pilot license starts with obtaining a private pilot license. If the candidate already holds a license from another ICAO member country, they can convert it within a period of around one month. Hours flown in Canada or other countries can be included in the calculation of the personalized training quote, as long as they are certified and meet the requirements set by Transport Canada.

This license allows for the addition of various qualifications, such as instrument flight, multi-engine, night flight, and others. These qualifications are essential for obtaining a job as a professional pilot. While the night flight qualification is usually included in professional flight training quotes, it may not be specifically mentioned.

    Prerequisites
    • 18 years old
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1
    Ground Training
    • Theoretical ground course 120 hrs
    • Preparatory instructions & presentation 30 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Practice flights in dual control flight 76 hours
    • Solo practice flights 127 hours

Glider Pilot License

Within the 12 months preceding the date of application for the licence, an applicant shall demonstrate in flight and on the ground familiarity with, and the ability to perform both normal and emergency manoeuvres appropriate to the glider used in the test and with a degree of competency appropriate to the holder of a pilot licence - glider.

An applicant who holds a pilot permit or licence in any other category of aircraft, except ultra-light aeroplanes, shall be deemed to have met 10 of the 15 hours ground school instruction requirement.An applicant who holds a pilot licence - aeroplane category shall have the total glider pilot flight training time reduced to a minimum of 3 hours, which shall include the minimum flight training specified.

    Prerequisites
    • 16 years old
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1,3 or 4
    Ground Training
    • Theoretical ground course 15 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • minimum of 6 hours of glider pilot flight training
    • Practice flights in dual control flight minimum 1 hours
    • Solo practice flights minimum 2 hours

Ratings

Multi-Engine

The multi-engine qualification allows you to acquire the knowledge necessary to fly in an aircraft with more than one engine. This qualification remains very popular because it makes it possible to fly and more efficient aircraft.

    Prerequisites
    • Private or Commercial Pilot License
    • Appropriate medical certificate
    Ground Training
    • Preparatory instruction course 5 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Practice flights in dual control flight 10 hrs
    • Flight test with TC: 1hr

Instrument Flight On Single-Engine (group 3)

The instrument flight rating equips the pilot with the ability to operate the aircraft in adverse weather conditions. It is a crucial requirement for those pursuing a career as a professional airline pilot. The training for this rating typically lasts for a minimum of 2 months.

    Prerequisites
    • Private or Commercial Pilot License
    • 50 hours of flight as Pilot in Command
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1 or 3
    Ground Training
    • Ground theory lessons (optional) 20 hrs
    • Preparatory instruction course 10 hrs
    • Simulator 10 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Dual control flight (professional): 15 hrs
    • Dual control flight (private): 35 hrs
    • Flight test with TC: 1.5 hr

Multi-Engine Instrument Flight (group 1)

This qualification offers the pilot of private or professional aircraft all possibilities in terms of learning and experience. It allows the pilot to perform flights at all times on larger aircraft. In the case of a multi-engine instrument flight rating, you must first have your multi-engine rating.

    Prerequisites
    • 18 years old
    • Private or Commercial Pilot License 50 hrs of flight as PIC
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1 or 3
    Ground Training
    • Ground theory lessons (optional) 20 hrs
    • Preparatory instructions and presentation 10 hrs
    • Flight simulator 5 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • dual control flight 15 hrs
    • dual control flight (Private) 35 hrs
    • Flight test with TC: 1.5 h

Night Flight

The night flight qualification is ideal for pilots who aim to increase their flight hours during winter, as reduced daylight hours can have an impact on their flight time. This qualification is part of the comprehensive commercial pilot license training

    Prerequisites
    • Private or Commercial Pilots License
    • Valid medical certificate of category 1 or 3
    Ground Training
    • Preparatory instruction course 2 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Night flights in dual control 5 hrs
    • Solo night flights
    • Instrument flights 11 hrs

Fight Instructor

This qualification makes it possible to become an instructor for the training of aspiring pilots in a flight school holding a Transport Canada operating certificate. You will also have the opportunity to amass more hours of flight, while remaining in the field of piloting. What could be better to perfect your piloting techniques than to teach them?

    Prerequisites
    • Commercial Pilots License
    • Valid Category 1 medical certificate
    Ground Training
    • Ground theory lessons and preparatory instructions 40 hrs
    • Manuals & materials
    Flight Training
    • Practice flights in dual control flight 30 hrs
    • Flight test with Transport Canada 1.5 h
